# Revorax Launch Readiness Blueprint

> **Historical baseline:** This large checklist began before the current pilot
> implementation. For the current product boundary, launch decision, fixes,
> pricing, competitive positioning, and exact next steps, read
> [`PRODUCT_LAUNCH_REVIEW.md`](PRODUCT_LAUNCH_REVIEW.md) first. Items below
> remain useful as the longer-term operating backlog.

**Version:** 1.0  
**Prepared:** 2026-07-18  
**Audience:** Founder, product, engineering, design, sales, customer success, security, and operations  
**Operating position:** Revorax is an **AI-assisted missed-call recovery product for HVAC teams**. It is not yet a live AI voice receptionist, autonomous dispatcher, or production booking system.

> This is an operating manual, not legal, tax, or telecommunications advice. Retain US telecom/privacy counsel before activating customer phone numbers or commercial automated messaging.

## Executive decision

**Current launch-readiness score: 28/100 - No-Go for public beta or paid self-serve.**

The repository contains a polished responsive marketing site, a real FastAPI/API and data-model foundation, signed Twilio webhook handling, voicemail transcription, AI qualification, lead APIs, and a single-service Railway deployment. It does **not** yet contain the customer-facing dashboard/onboarding experience, durable worker system, SMS consent and opt-out controls, billing/entitlements, automated tests/CI, or the safety controls required for paid production traffic.

| Launch tier | Decision | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Internal demo | Go | Landing page, test account, development/test numbers only. |
| Concierge alpha | Conditional Go | 1-3 manually approved HVAC businesses, test/approved numbers, founder monitoring, written pilot agreement, no public sign-up. |
| Closed paid beta | No-Go until P0s close | 3-10 contracted design partners after compliance, worker/idempotency, dashboard, security, and testing gates. |
| Public/self-serve | No-Go | Requires all beta gates plus billing, support, legal, security, and operational readiness. |

## How to use this checklist

Every item follows the same compact format so it can be operated as a backlog:

`Purpose / importance` states why it exists. `Dependencies / order` gives prerequisites and the recommended sequence. `Done` is the completion criterion. `Mistake / best practice` prevents common failure. `Priority / owner / effort` uses Critical, High, Medium, Low and an accountable owner.

### Phase order

| Phase | Outcome | Exit condition |
|---|---|---|
| 0. Truth and focus | One narrow promise, ICP, and pilot offer | Founder approves the product boundary and no marketing exceeds it. |
| 1. Safety foundation | Legal, security, consent, tenancy, and deployment controls | All Critical controls are tested in staging. |
| 2. Complete vertical slice | An approved business can complete the full missed-call recovery journey | Test call to human follow-up is durable, observable, and consent-safe. |
| 3. Closed beta | 3-10 paid design partners | Weekly outcomes, support, and retention evidence exist. |
| 4. Controlled public launch | Repeatable onboarding and support | Go/No-Go criteria are met for 30 consecutive days. |
| 5. Scale | Multi-location, integrations, and automation | Core unit economics and reliability are stable. |

## Current-state evidence and non-negotiables

- The marketing site is strong, but `frontend/app` has no authenticated product UI; it exposes only the homepage and demo-request route.
- The backend has authentication, business, lead, call, SMS, appointment, and stats APIs, but no frontend consumer for them.
- Webhooks currently run transcription, LLM, SMS, and email synchronously. Build a queue/outbox before real traffic.
- A user can currently sign up and trigger Twilio number provisioning without verification, payment, or an approval gate. Keep provisioning manual/invite-only until fixed.
- SMS must not be launched until consent, STOP/START/HELP, suppression, A2P registration, and message delivery observability exist. Twilio requires consent and A2P 10DLC registration for applicable US long-code application messaging. [Twilio A2P guide](https://www.twilio.com/docs/messaging/compliance/a2p-10dlc) and [Messaging Policy](https://www.twilio.com/en-us/legal/messaging-policy).
- A known cross-tenant appointment/lead mutation must be fixed before external users. Every tenant-scoped query and write needs a regression test.
- Do not claim live answering, dispatch-calendar booking, response-time guarantees, revenue lift, or universal ROI until those capabilities and measurements exist.

## 1. Company

- [ ] **Vision** - **Purpose / importance:** Define the future state: every HVAC caller receives a fast, useful human-path response; keeps product decisions focused. **Dependencies / order:** Founder workshop in Phase 0 before roadmap. **Done:** One sentence approved and used in planning: "Every HVAC caller gets a clear next step, even when the team cannot answer." **Mistake / best practice:** Do not equate vision with feature list; use it to reject unrelated CRM/voice-agent scope. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Founder / 2h.
- [ ] **Mission** - **Purpose / importance:** State the present job: recover missed HVAC demand with a qualified, visible handoff. **Dependencies / order:** Vision, then positioning. **Done:** A measurable mission in the website, sales deck, and onboarding. **Mistake / best practice:** Avoid "replace dispatchers" messaging; promise a better first recovery step. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Founder + Product / 2h.
- [ ] **Core values** - **Purpose / importance:** Set decision rules for AI, privacy, and support. **Dependencies / order:** Mission before hiring/support SOPs. **Done:** 4-5 observable values: customer control, truthful automation, urgency-aware service, privacy by design, ownership. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not publish generic values; attach one behavior to each. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Founder / 0.5d.
- [ ] **Company story** - **Purpose / importance:** Give HVAC owners a credible reason Revorax exists. **Dependencies / order:** Customer interviews before copy. **Done:** 150-word founder story rooted in missed-call leakage and human handoff. **Mistake / best practice:** Never invent origin/customer stories; use interview-approved language. **Priority / owner / effort:** Medium / Founder + Marketing / 0.5d.
- [ ] **Positioning** - **Purpose / importance:** Own a narrow category against full field-service systems and AI phone agents. **Dependencies / order:** Competitor map, then website/sales copy. **Done:** "AI-assisted missed-call recovery for HVAC teams" is used consistently. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not market as an all-in-one AI receptionist or booking engine today. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Founder + Product Marketing / 1d.
- [ ] **USP** - **Purpose / importance:** Make the wedge defensible: platform-agnostic recovery, qualified context, and human handoff. **Dependencies / order:** Validate with 10 interviews and pilot data. **Done:** Three proof-backed differentiators with a demonstration for each. **Mistake / best practice:** "AI" and auto-text alone are not durable differentiators; lead with recovered outcome evidence. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Founder + Sales / 2d.
- [ ] **Target ICP** - **Purpose / importance:** Prevent expensive broad-market selling. **Dependencies / order:** Competitor exclusions and 20 discovery calls. **Done:** Initial ICP: independent residential-service HVAC, 3-15 technicians, one location, meaningful missed-call leakage, dispatcher/owner-led follow-up, and no active native AI call agent. **Mistake / best practice:** Disqualify accounts already using ServiceTitan/Workiz/Podium/Housecall Pro AI equivalents unless a gap is proven. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Sales + Product / 2d.
- [ ] **Customer personas** - **Purpose / importance:** Align product and sales around buyer, user, and impacted caller. **Dependencies / order:** ICP, interviews, call observation. **Done:** Owner/GM buyer, dispatcher/CSR user, technician stakeholder, and caller journey documented with pains, success, and objections. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not treat the owner and dispatcher as the same persona. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Product + Sales / 2d.
- [ ] **Brand promise** - **Purpose / importance:** Set an honest, testable expectation. **Dependencies / order:** Positioning and vertical-slice proof. **Done:** "A clearer next step for every missed HVAC call" with product limits documented. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not promise that no lead is ever lost. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Founder + Design / 0.5d.
- [ ] **Elevator pitch** - **Purpose / importance:** Make outreach and demos consistent. **Dependencies / order:** Positioning and ICP. **Done:** 30-second and 90-second versions tested with five owners. **Mistake / best practice:** Start with business leakage, not model/provider names. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Sales / 0.5d.
- [ ] **Revenue model** - **Purpose / importance:** Match price to customer value and unit costs. **Dependencies / order:** Pilot usage model, telecom/AI cost model. **Done:** Per-location recurring subscription, fair-use definition, and overage policy approved. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not bill raw rings or opaque AI tokens; bill eligible recovery workflows/location. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Founder + Finance / 1d.
- [ ] **Pricing** - **Purpose / importance:** Create a sellable, honest launch offer. **Dependencies / order:** Pilot cost model and willingness-to-pay interviews. **Done:** Founder Pilot $99/location/month (first 10, 90-day rate lock), Start $99/100 eligible workflows, Grow $199/400 workflows; revise from measured margin. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not charge full AI-voice-agent pricing before live voice/booking exists. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Founder + Sales / 1d.
- [ ] **Business model** - **Purpose / importance:** Define service boundaries and how value compounds. **Dependencies / order:** Revenue model, support capacity. **Done:** White-glove onboarding plus recurring software, manual pilot support, and one-location scope recorded in contracts. **Mistake / best practice:** Avoid hidden concierge work that makes margins unknowable; log service hours. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / COO + Founder / 1d.
- [ ] **Market validation** - **Purpose / importance:** Prove a painful, paid problem before scaling. **Dependencies / order:** ICP and audit script. **Done:** 20 discovery calls, 10 missed-call audits, 3 paid pilots, and at least one customer-verified recovery case study. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not count compliments or demo requests as validation. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Founder + Sales / 4-6w.
- [ ] **Competitor analysis** - **Purpose / importance:** Guide qualification and differentiation. **Dependencies / order:** Current market scan, quarterly refresh. **Done:** Feature/pricing/positioning battlecard for ServiceTitan Voice Agent, Housecall Pro CSR AI, Workiz Genius Answering, Podium Phones/AI Employee, Airvvy, Reply, and Goodcall. **Mistake / best practice:** Never copy unverified competitor claims; record source date/URL. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Product Marketing / 2d quarterly.
- [ ] **SWOT analysis** - **Purpose / importance:** Turn assumptions into risks and choices. **Dependencies / order:** Competitor analysis and pilot feedback. **Done:** Quarterly SWOT with each weakness/threat mapped to an owner/action. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not treat SWOT as a slide; put risks into the roadmap. **Priority / owner / effort:** Medium / Leadership / 0.5d quarterly.

- [ ] **MRR** - **Purpose / importance:** Track recurring run-rate. **Dependencies / order:** Subscription ledger. **Done:** New/expansion/contraction/churn/reactivation MRR roll-forward monthly. **Mistake / best practice:** Exclude one-time setup/consulting from MRR. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Finance / 1d.
- [ ] **ARR** - **Purpose / importance:** Communicate annualized recurring trajectory. **Dependencies / order:** Reliable MRR. **Done:** ARR is MRR x 12 with clear caveat for early pilots. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not annualize temporary pilots as durable contracted ARR. **Priority / owner / effort:** Medium / Finance / 0.5d.
- [ ] **CAC** - **Purpose / importance:** Decide which acquisition motions can scale. **Dependencies / order:** Channel attribution and fully loaded spend. **Done:** CAC by channel/cohort including tools, paid spend, sales time, and founder effort estimate. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not claim a low CAC by omitting labor. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Finance + Growth / 1d.
- [ ] **LTV** - **Purpose / importance:** Set spend/retention priorities. **Dependencies / order:** Margin and churn data. **Done:** Conservative gross-margin LTV model with sensitivity bands, not a single optimistic number. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not calculate LTV from fewer than several cohorts as fact. **Priority / owner / effort:** Medium / Finance / 1d.
- [ ] **Burn rate** - **Purpose / importance:** Preserve runway to reach repeatable retention. **Dependencies / order:** Cash forecast. **Done:** Monthly net burn, cash balance, runway, scenario plan, and spending approval thresholds. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not count undrawn commitments as runway. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Founder + Finance / weekly.

## 24. Scalability

- [ ] **Multi-tenant** - **Purpose / importance:** Scale customers without compromising isolation. **Dependencies / order:** Tenant-safe schema/RBAC/tests. **Done:** Proven tenant boundaries, tenant configuration, quotas, per-tenant audit, and isolation monitoring. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not add customers before tenant tests exist. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Backend + Security / Phase 1.
- [ ] **Multi-location** - **Purpose / importance:** Support larger HVAC operations after one-location value is proven. **Dependencies / order:** Location model, number routing, permissions, reporting. **Done:** Location entity, numbers/hours/services/alerts per location, role scope, consolidated/separate reporting. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not overload one business record with comma-separated locations. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Product + Backend / Post-PMF.
- [ ] **White label** - **Purpose / importance:** Enable partner/channel distribution only when operationally justified. **Dependencies / order:** Multi-tenant/branding/support/legal. **Done:** Brand domains, theming, billing/support boundaries, data access, and contractual model documented. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not white-label before the core UX/support is stable. **Priority / owner / effort:** Low / Product + Partnerships / Later.
- [ ] **API** - **Purpose / importance:** Support integrations and future platform value. **Dependencies / order:** Stable internal API/auth/RBAC/rate limits. **Done:** Public API scope, OAuth/API key strategy, docs, quotas, audit, versioning, and support policy. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not expose internal endpoints as public API by accident. **Priority / owner / effort:** Medium / Backend + Product / Post-PMF.
- [ ] **Integrations** - **Purpose / importance:** Fit workflows without becoming a services company. **Dependencies / order:** Pilot demand and one connector architecture. **Done:** Prioritized integration roadmap from pilot data; one high-demand FSM/calendar connector is production-grade. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not build five integrations based only on sales asks. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Product + Integrations / Post-beta.
- [ ] **Plugin system** - **Purpose / importance:** Allow extension once stable primitives exist. **Dependencies / order:** Public API/events/permissions/security. **Done:** Sandboxed extension model, review/approval, lifecycle, tenant permissions, and observability. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not create arbitrary code execution in customer tenancy. **Priority / owner / effort:** Low / Platform + Security / Later.
- [ ] **Enterprise features** - **Purpose / importance:** Meet larger buyer requirements after core reliability. **Dependencies / order:** RBAC, security baseline, multi-location, support process. **Done:** SSO/SAML, SCIM, audit export, SLA, DPA, data residency/retention controls, and procurement pack only when supportable. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not promise SOC 2/enterprise controls without evidence. **Priority / owner / effort:** Low now / Founder + Security / Later.

## 25. Launch

- [ ] **Internal Alpha** - **Purpose / importance:** Prove technical flow before customer exposure. **Dependencies / order:** Phase 1 safety controls and test numbers. **Done:** Team executes 30 scripted call/SMS/STOP/retry/provider-failure journeys; all Critical defects are fixed or consciously blocked. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not use public/customer numbers for first tests. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Engineering + Product / 1w.
- [ ] **Closed Beta** - **Purpose / importance:** Validate real operations with controlled risk. **Dependencies / order:** Complete vertical slice, contracts/consent, support/on-call, staging/monitoring. **Done:** 3-10 approved paid design partners, weekly review, no unresolved Critical incident, outcome data, and explicit pilot boundaries. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not open a waitlist as a beta if every signup can provision a number. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Founder + COO / 4-8w.
- [ ] **Public Beta** - **Purpose / importance:** Test repeatable onboarding/retention beyond concierge mode. **Dependencies / order:** Closed-beta gates, billing/entitlements, dashboard, legal, support. **Done:** Automated but guarded onboarding, payment/approval, self-service support, all P0s closed, and 30 days stable. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not confuse a polished marketing site with public-beta readiness. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Leadership / after beta.
- [ ] **First customer** - **Purpose / importance:** Create one deeply successful, referenceable outcome. **Dependencies / order:** Pilot offer and operational readiness. **Done:** Contract, baseline, configuration, first recovery event, verified human follow-up, weekly success review, and permission path for proof. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not optimize for logo value over ICP fit. **Priority / owner / effort:** Critical / Founder + Customer Success / immediate.
- [ ] **First 10 customers** - **Purpose / importance:** Test repeatability across similar operators. **Dependencies / order:** Successful first customer, onboarding/SOP/monitoring. **Done:** 10 paying locations with activation/retention/outcome cohorts and support load understood. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not expand to many states/segments before a coherent cohort exists. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Sales + Customer Success / 3-6m.
- [ ] **First 100 customers** - **Purpose / importance:** Prove scalable economics and operations. **Dependencies / order:** Product-market fit, automation, support/billing/DevOps scale. **Done:** Reliable retention, healthy margin, repeatable CAC/payback, multi-person on-call/support, and resilience testing. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not staff/grow to 100 while core SMS/worker compliance is manual. **Priority / owner / effort:** Medium / Leadership / after PMF.
- [ ] **Feedback loop** - **Purpose / importance:** Convert live use into product learning. **Dependencies / order:** Instrumentation, customer cadence. **Done:** Weekly customer calls, biweekly product review, issue/idea taxonomy, decision log, and feedback-to-release communication. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not let sales requests override outcome data without a clear decision. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Product + Customer Success / ongoing.
- [ ] **Product iteration** - **Purpose / importance:** Improve activation and retention without destabilizing core flow. **Dependencies / order:** Metrics, tests, release process. **Done:** Hypothesis backlog, experiment/rollout plan, measurable success, rollback, and release notes. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not ship major changes directly to every pilot. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Product + Engineering / ongoing.
- [ ] **Growth plan** - **Purpose / importance:** Scale only after retention and operational capacity. **Dependencies / order:** PMF metrics, financial plan, channel proof. **Done:** Quarterly growth model by target metro/ICP/channel/capacity, hiring plan, budget, risk gates, and stop conditions. **Mistake / best practice:** Do not grow pipeline faster than onboarding/support can responsibly absorb. **Priority / owner / effort:** High / Founder + Growth + Finance / quarterly.

# Complete launch roadmap

## Weeks 0-2: make the promise safe and true

1. Freeze public self-serve signup and Twilio number provisioning; switch to invite-only/manual approval.
2. Fix the cross-tenant appointment/lead mutation and create regression tests.
3. Add production configuration validation: `APP_ENV=production`, `DEBUG=false`, high-entropy JWT secret, valid public URLs, database, approved CORS, and nonempty required provider secrets; fail closed.
4. Design/implement consent ledger, STOP/START/HELP branching, suppression, and A2P/Message Service onboarding path. Engage counsel.
5. Add Privacy, Terms, SMS terms, AI disclosure, and data-request contact. Do not represent legal compliance without counsel review.
6. Define the truthful MVP and rewrite any claim that exceeds missed-call recovery/human handoff.

## Weeks 2-5: build one durable vertical slice

1. Build worker/outbox/idempotency architecture with Redis/ARQ or an equivalent managed queue.
2. Make Twilio callbacks acknowledge quickly and return valid TwiML where the voice flow requires it.
3. Add duplicate/replay protection for call, SMS, notification, and payment events.
4. Build the authenticated dashboard, onboarding/settings, and internal operator view.
5. Add structured LLM output schema, guardrails, fallback, human escalation, and evaluation suite.
6. Add test suite, CI, staging environment, logs/metrics/alerts, and container smoke test.

## Weeks 5-8: instrument and run a concierge alpha

1. Run 30+ internal/rehearsal phone/SMS journeys, including STOP, duplicate callbacks, provider errors, and database/queue recovery.
2. Approve 1-3 HVAC businesses manually; establish call baseline and written pilot scope.
3. Onboard each business with a real test call, approved sender, hours/services, owner escalation path, and weekly success review.
4. Capture recovered workflow metrics, human corrections, delivery outcomes, and customer-verified appointments/revenue.
5. Run restore drill, security review, UAT, and support escalation drill.

## Weeks 8-12: closed paid beta

1. Add invoice/manual-contract or tested Stripe billing/entitlement path.
2. Enroll 3-10 paid design partners in one metro/ICP cohort.
3. Publish one verified case study, refine pricing/offer, and continue founder-led missed-call audits.
4. Review every Critical/High item weekly; pause onboarding if on-call, delivery, consent, or tenant-safety indicators fail.

## After PMF: controlled scale

1. Prove 90-day retention and healthy contribution margin for the initial cohort.
2. Build one demanded calendar/FSM integration and outcome attribution.
3. Add multi-location/RBAC, then enterprise or live-voice features only with a documented safety/reliability case.

# Critical path

```text
Truthful MVP + legal entity
        -> telecom/privacy counsel + data map
        -> consent/A2P/STOP controls + manual provisioning gate
        -> tenant safety + production config + test suite
        -> durable worker/outbox + Twilio end-to-end flow
        -> dashboard/onboarding + monitoring/on-call
        -> internal alpha -> paid design partners -> retention proof
        -> billing/self-serve readiness -> controlled public beta
```

The immediate hard gates are: tenant isolation, production secrets/config, manual/verified Twilio provisioning, durable webhook work, valid TwiML/voice behavior, A2P/opt-out compliance, legal pages/contracts, real dashboard, tests/CI, backup/restore, and an on-call/support owner.

# Go / No-Go criteria

## Go for internal alpha only when

- Every core callback is signed and tested with test numbers.
- No real customer number is provisioned automatically.
- The team can run a complete missed-call/voicemail/SMS/STOP/retry simulation.
- Production environment has no placeholder/default secrets and alerts route to a named owner.

## Go for closed paid beta only when

- All Critical security, tenant-isolation, queue/idempotency, consent/opt-out, and legal items are complete and tested.
- A2P sender/campaign strategy and customer consent process are approved by counsel and operationally implemented.
- The authenticated dashboard and onboarding are usable by a dispatcher without developer help.
- A provider failure gives a safe fallback/human escalation, not silent loss or duplicate sends.
- Database backup and restore drill has passed; monitoring/alerting/on-call work in staging.
- A written pilot contract, support escalation, data-processing/privacy terms, and payment/approval gate exist.
- 3 pilot users complete UAT and no open Critical defect remains.

## Go for public beta only when

- Closed beta has sustained 30 days with no unresolved P0/P1 operational or compliance incident.
- Activation, recovery response, delivery, qualified lead, human follow-up, and retention metrics meet target thresholds.
- Billing/entitlement, account lifecycle, support, customer data rights, and release/rollback processes are operating.
- Leadership signs the risk register and capacity plan.

# Post-launch checklist

## First 7 days

- Hold daily operations review: webhook success, queue age, SMS delivery/opt-outs, provider cost, incidents, and support tickets.
- Interview each active pilot; verify each recovered lead and customer-reported outcome.
- Pause onboarding immediately for any consent, tenant, duplicate-send, or critical security issue.
- Ship only safe, reversible fixes; document every incident and customer communication.

## First 30 days

- Produce cohort baseline-to-outcome report and first verified case study candidate.
- Review plan margin, support load, onboarding time, and delivery/AI quality.
- Run first restore and incident tabletop drill; complete access review and dependency update review.
- Decide whether the biggest bottleneck is product reliability, activation, demand, or retention before adding features.

## First 90 days

- Measure logo/revenue retention, activation, recovered-workflow rate, customer-verified bookings/revenue, gross margin, and NPS/qualitative trust.
- Hold a roadmap summit using real cohort evidence; choose one integration or multi-location investment only if demand/retention warrants it.
- Renew/expand satisfied pilots, retire unprofitable service work, and establish a quarterly security/privacy/DR review.
